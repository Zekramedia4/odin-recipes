Orange Chicken

Description

Orange Chicken is a delight for the senses. You've got the crunch of the crispy fried chicken, covered with a sweet, tangy, and umami sauce, a little spice, and a wonderful smell that will fill the house.

Though often thought to have Chinese origins (there may be some truth to that) it is almost entirely an American invention with some Chinese ingredients and ideas. Orange chicken has been popularized by the fast food chain Panda Express.

Ingredients

Chicken Dredge:

  • 1.5lbs Chicken thighs
  • 1 egg
  • Salt
  • Msg
  • 1 cup (140g) corn starch
  • 1/2 cup (75g) all purpose flour

Sauce:

  • 3/4 cup (170g) sugar
  • 3/4 cup (180ml) white vinegar
  • 1/3 cup + 1 tbsp (90ml) double fermented soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) water
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • Splash saixing wine
  • Splash Chinkian (black) vinegar
  • 1 tbsp (10g) corn starch + 1 tbsp water
  • 2 inch knob ginger, grated
  • 1-2 Red fresno chilies, finely minced

Steps

For the sauce:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine sugar, white vinegra, soy sauce, water, and zest and juice of an orange. Whisk together and bring to a boil over high heat.
  2. Reduce to medium heat once boiling, and let simmer while optionally adding saixing wine and chinkian vinegar. Simmer for 8-10 minutes.
  3. Separately mix together cornstarch and water then whisk into the hot sauce for an additional minute or until thickened.
  4. Immediately add grated garlic and ginger, and finely minced fresno chilites. Whisk and let steep.

For the chicken:

  1. Cut the chicken into bite sized pieces and lightly season with salt.
  2. In a medium size bowl, whisk togehter cornstarch and all-purpose flour.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs with a splash of water.
  4. Toss the chicken pieces into the flour mixture then egg mixture, and then back into the flour mixture. Place to the side and rinse and repeat.
  5. fill a heavy bottom pot with frying oil 2-3" high and heat to 375F.
  6. Fry chicken in batches for 3-5 minutes and cool on a wire rack.
  7. Toss chicken and the sauce in a bowl until fully coated.
  8. Serve over rice, and top with green onions and sliced fresh fresno chilies
